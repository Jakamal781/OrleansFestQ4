Spirited Must-Do Attractions

Haunted Tours

Learn about the haunted history of the French Quarter with a guided tour of its spooky past or make a visit to one of the historic New Orleans cemeteries.


The Mortuary

Meet the legends of the spirits who came for burial and never left. Haunted tours take place all year long and during the Halloween season the 1872 mansion turns into a haunted house experience unlike any other. The former funeral home takes on a new spooky theme each year.

Krewe of Boo
October 22

The official New Orleans Halloween parade frightens and excites with ghoulish floats and spooky characters.


Voodoo Music and Arts Experience
October 28 - 30

Arcade Fire, The Weekend and Band of Horses headline one of New Orleans' most popular festivals. The spooky event starts before sundown and continues all night. Check out roller coasters, food stands and countless Louisiana classics.

Holiday Fun Around the City

Celebration in The Oaks
November 25 - January 3

A family friendly month-long event showcases the holiday season in one of the country's most popular light displays. Thousands of beautiful lights illuminate the outdoor displays.

Krewe of Jingle
December 3

Delight in floats and marching bands as the parade works through downtown New Orleans. Then enjoy live music on Harrah's Canal Street Plaza.

Jackson Square Caroling
December 18

Spread some cheer in historic Jackson Square with the help of hundreds of candles and the tunes you know and love. The joy of the carolers makes this a not-to-miss tradition that goes back to 1946.

Fulton Street Lights Up To Make Spirits Bright

Miracle on Fulton Street
December 2 - 24

Stroll through a winter wonderland complete with interactive ornaments, twinkling lights and daily "snow" fall. Each weekend enjoy visits with Santa and his elves, live music and more.

Running of the Santas
December 17

Enjoy a festive pub crawl with thousands of fellow Santa-costumed partiers. This charity event starts at the South Pole located at Manning's and moves to the North Pole with plenty of cocktail stops along the way.

Football Is A Good Call

No Place like Dome

Root on your hometown team as they play the black & gold at the Superdome. The stadium is footsteps away from our hotel and there are plenty of tailgating and tablegating spots in between.

Spend the Day with Football Royalty

Hang at Harrah's for pro and college games all season. Manning's offers 30 TVs, including two 13-foot mega screens, and elevated tailgate fare like the Archie Burger and a tasty Louisiana shrimp po'boy. Wash it down it with one of the 24 beers on tap. Watch the game from a comfortable leather recliner or cheer from the French Quarter-style courtyard. Archie, Peyton or Eli might even stop by to join you.

Exclusive Watch Parties

Be part of the professional football game day action at Masquerade. A complimentary tailgate experience is a great way to catch the game when the black & gold are playing.
